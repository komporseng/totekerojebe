Erika Andreeva is a Russian tennis player who has a Women’s Tennis Association (WTA) ranking of world no. 121. She achieved her career-high ranking of no. 114 on 24 April 2023.

Moreover, she currently ranks no. 466 in the WTA doubles ranking. She is a right-handed (two-handed backhand) player. As a junior, she finished as a runner-up at the 2021 French Open in the girl’s singles event but lost to Linda Nosková in the final.

Andreeva has won three singles titles and one doubles title on the ITF Circuit throughout her career. She won her first senior ITF Circuit title at the $15k event in Pazardzhik, Bulgaria in November 2020.

Just after a month, she won her second ITF title at the $15k Cairo, Egypt, and her third was in March 2021. Likewise, she earned her doubles title on the ITF Circuit at Verbier Open, Switzerland in August 2021.

Erika made her WTA Tour debut at the 2022 Ladies Open Lausanne where she recorded her first WTA Tour-level win after dropping only three games against Anna Blinkova in the first round. In August 2022, she made her grand slam debut at the 2022 US Open.

In 2023, she competed in the 2023 ATX Open in Austin, Texas, and received a wildcard into the main draw of the 2023 Miami Open.
