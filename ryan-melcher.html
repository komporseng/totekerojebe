    Ryan Melcher Father, Terry Melcher

    Terry Melcher (born Terrence Paul Jorden) was an American record producer, singer, and songwriter who was instrumental in shaping the mid-to-late 1960s California Sound and folk-rock movements.

    His best-known contributions were producing Byrds’ first two albums Mr. Tambourine Man (1965) and Turn! Turn! Turn! (1965), as well as most of the hit recordings of Paul Revere & the Raiders and Gentle Soul.

    He is also known for his brief association with Charles mansion, a songwriter and cult leader who was later convicted of several murders. Melcher was the only child of actress/singer Doris Day; his father was Doris’ first husband Al Jordan, and he was adopted by her third husband Martin Melcher.

    Most of his early recordings were with the vocal surf acts the Rip Chords and Bruce & Terry. In the 1960s, Melcher was familiarized with the Beach Boys, helping connect Brian Wilson to Smile lyricist Van Dyke Parks. Melcher later produced several singles for the Beach Boys in the 1980s and the 1990s, including ”Kokomo” (1988), which topped U.S. record charts.
