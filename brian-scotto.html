What is Brian Scotto’s net worth? Who is Brian Scotto’s wife?

Introduction

Brian Scotto is an American motorsports fan who became the marketing editor for Monster World Rally Team and the chief brand officer for the motorsports lifestyle brand Hoonigan.

Scotto has also been the creative mind and driving force behind all Gymkhana videos – the coolest, craziest, most viral automotive content ever put online.

Biography

He was born on December 1, 1980, in New York City, USA.

Brian lived in New York City before moving to Venice, California.

Scotto attended the University of Vermont (a public research university in Burlington that was founded in 1791) and Stony Brook University (one of 4 university centers of the State University of New York system), but, he didn’t finish any.

During an interview, he said:

”I studied mechanical engineering and wasn’t really sure what I would do with that after college, so it really was a difficult period for me.”

He became an automotive journalist for 0-60 Magazine. Scotto served as editor-in-chief of 0-60 Magazine for over 4 years, until December 2010.

In 2010, Scotto and Ken Block joined forces to create Hoonigan, a brand that has now become synonymous with the lifestyle of car fanatics worldwide.

Brian is Hoonigan’s chief creative officer. Furthermore, Scotto has been behind the camera for the duration of Gymkhana’s series.

During an interview, Brian said:

”The concept for Hoonigan grew out of Ken Block and myself recognizing a void in the world of motorsports, there were no brands like DC Shoes for car guys.”

Note

Ken Block is a professional race car driver who began his rallying career with the Vermont SportsCar team in 2005. In the same year, Ken was named Rally’s Rookie of the Year.

Block, together with Matt LeBlanc (actor and television host), while filming a segment for the show Top Gear, performed donuts at the Cenotaph War Memorial in London in a Ford Mustang.

Over the years, Block has competed in various action sports events, including motocross, snowboarding, and skateboarding.

Brian helped create the popular web series “Field Trip,” which airs on the Hoonigan YouTube channel.

”So there was all this emphasis around Gymkhana grid and the course, so the idea of doing the ultimate Gymkhana grid course came out.”
