Introduction to Soccer

Like every athlete, William Yarbrough commenced playing at his early age. Indeed, he had a keen interest in the game and was often playing along with his siblings in his house’s backyard.

William Yarbrough | Career

However, it was only after five years; he joined the senior team of Pachuca in 2010, as he played for their youth team. Initially, he started his senior team’s career with the loan to Jaibos Tampico Madero.

During his tenure, he appeared in the Liga Premier de México 2010–2011 season. The following year, he was again loaned to Titanes Tulancingo. 

León

At first, Yarbrough featured Leon in loan while being with Pachuca. He only played in the first round of the Copa MX Apertura against Dorados de Sinaloa during his loan phase.

With the end of the season, he moved to Leon as a starting goalkeeper, as he contributed to bag two Liga MX titles.

Right then, he also led all the Liga MX goalkeepers in both saves and clean sheets. However, the smooth ride produced a bump when Leon acquired Rodolfo Cota in 2018. 

From then, he had few appearances; four matches in 2018 and two in 2019.

Colorado Rapids

Looking back to his loan days in Rapids, he had his debut over Sporting Kansas City at the MLS is Back Tournament.

Thus, when the thought or passion for playing for MLS first stroke him, he wanted to continue it. With the commencement in MLS, he watched four or five games a weekend.
